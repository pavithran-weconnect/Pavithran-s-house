Project Intent

Luxury presentation, practical construction brief

The building should combine a strong commercial frontage with a clearly separated private home entrance. The residence should feel broad, bright, premium and easy to maintain, with every floor planned around daily usability, ventilation, privacy and service access.

Architectural Direction

Modern luxury elevation, broad approach, large porte cochere feel, garden edges and clean exterior lighting.

Functional Direction

Basement parking, lift and staircase connection, floor-wise storage, smart systems and maintenance-friendly services.

Interior Direction

Warm minimal palette, high ceilings where possible, marble/stone accents, concealed storage and refined lighting.

Design Mood

Image-led references for the builder and architect

Material + Light Vision

Natural lighting, higher broader spaces and calm matte finishes

01

Natural lighting

Prioritize larger openings, light wells, courtyard edges, skylights and soft indirect lighting so the home feels bright in the day and warm at night.

02

Higher and broader space

Where structure and approval allow, use high ceilings, wider passages, generous lobbies and open family zones instead of tight room-by-room planning.

03

Matte premium look

Use matte stone, satin metal, soft wood texture, muted wall finishes and concealed lighting so the house feels rich without glossy or loud finishes.

Creative Mindset

The house should feel imaginative, disciplined and builder-ready

01

Creative but controlled

The design can be bold and unique, but every curve, pattern, light strip and material change must connect to one approved theme.

02

Furniture included in cost

Built-in furniture, loose furniture, wardrobes, sofas, beds, dining, office furniture, outdoor seating and supermarket fixtures must be included in the cost plan.

03

Rich natural experience

Natural light, broader rooms, higher ceiling moments, matte finishes and warm indirect lighting should make the house feel calm, premium and spacious.

04

Proof before execution

Before work starts, the builder must provide drawings, material samples, furniture schedules, stage-wise cost and quality checkpoints for approval.

Floor Scope

Requirements by level

Ground Floor

Commercial frontage and private arrival

Supermarket frontage, secure private entry, biometric access, lift/stair core, reception, shoe storage and hidden washroom planning.

Basement

Parking, wash bay and maintenance store

Car parking, pressure washing bay, maintenance storage, small seating if possible and safe circulation to the house core.

First Floor

Main family living and kitchen level

High ceiling living, 100 inch TV, centralized sound, dry/wet kitchen, pantry, pooja, optional bedroom and automation.
